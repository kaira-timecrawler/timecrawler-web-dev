# The First Step

Three days had passed since her arrival in 1812. Three days in which Kaira had learned to pretend, to adapt, to survive. The ship's captain, a weather-beaten man named Don Alejandro, had taken her under his protection after she told him an invented story about being a traveler who had lost her luggage.

But Kaira wasn't worried about her present situation. She was worried about how to return.

Every night, before sleeping, she tried to repeat what she had done the first time. Close her eyes, concentrate, wish to go home. But nothing worked. Time didn't respond to her orders.

It was on the fourth night, during a storm, that she understood.

The ship rocked violently. Sailors ran across the deck, shouting orders. Kaira clung to the mast, terrified. And then, in the midst of chaos, she felt that familiar vibration inside. She hadn't sought it. It had come on its own.

*"It's not will"*, she thought as lightning illuminated the sea. *"It's necessity."*

She closed her eyes and concentrated, not on going home, but on being safe. On escaping the storm. On anywhere but that ship.

When she opened her eyes, she was in Paris. Year 1920. Jazz music played from a nearby café.

She had taken her first conscious step.
