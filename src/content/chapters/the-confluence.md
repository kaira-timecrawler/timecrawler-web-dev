# The Confluence

Paris, 1920, was a whirlwind of art, music, and excess. Kaira spent weeks exploring the city, learning to survive in each era with the same ease she learned to order food in unfamiliar restaurants. But there was always one question that tormented her: was she the only one?

The answer came one rainy afternoon in Montmartre.

She was sheltering under a bookstore awning when she saw him. A man of about forty, dressed in a suit that didn't quite fit the era. Too modern in details, too old in cut. And his eyes... his eyes had the same look Kaira had seen in the mirror every morning since her first jump.

The man stopped in front of her and smiled.

*"You feel it too, don't you?"* he asked in perfect French, but with an accent Kaira recognized immediately: twenty-first century Spanish.

*"Feel what?"*

*"The pull. The vibration. The certainty that you don't belong here, but you don't belong there either."*

Kaira took a step back. *"Who are you?"*

*"My name is Marcus. And I'm like you: a time walker."*

It was the first time she heard that word. Time walker. Someone who walks between eras.

*"You're not alone, Kaira"*, Marcus continued. *"There are more of us. But there's something you need to know: someone is looking for time walkers. Someone who shouldn't know we exist."*

The rain stopped. Kaira felt the vibration inside, stronger than ever.

*"I have to go"*, Marcus said. *"But we'll meet again. If you survive."*

And he disappeared into the Paris crowd, leaving Kaira with more questions than answers.
