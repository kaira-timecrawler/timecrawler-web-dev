# The Awakening

Kaira opened her eyes and knew something had changed. The air smelled of salt and old wood, nothing like the roasted coffee aroma of her kitchen. She sat up slowly, her hands resting on wooden cutlery worn by decades of use.

Through the window, the port of Cadiz bustled with activity. Sailing ships, fishermen, shouts of sailors. The clock tower showed seven in the morning, but there were no smartphones, no cars, no traffic noise.

Kaira whispered to herself: *"What year am I in?"*

A voice behind her answered: *"Year 1812, miss. And you should be on deck if you don't want the captain to put you on rations."*

She turned sharply. A man of about fifty, dressed in nineteenth-century sailor's clothes, watched her with curiosity. There was no fear in his gaze, only the confusion of finding a young woman in the boatswain's cabin.

*"I don't know how I got here"*, Kaira said, and it was true. The last thing she remembered was falling asleep in her Madrid apartment, in 2026.

The sailor shrugged. *"Seasickness, surely. Come, go up on deck. Today they sail to the Americas, and the captain tolerates no delays."*

Kaira obeyed, still dazed. As she climbed the wooden stairs, she felt something strange inside. A vibration, a certainty: time had called her, and she had answered. But why? And toward where?
